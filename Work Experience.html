Bookseller, Waterstones St. Albans

I worked as a bookseller at my local Waterstones store during my alevels. not only did this help me develop time management and organization skills due to studying five days a week then working in additional two days on the weekend, But I also gained some invaluable skills from my time spent interacting with customers, organizing the store and working closely with colleagues. My time at Waterstones was extremely fulfilling and provided me with a great basis for entering a working environment. I was trusted with keys to open and close the store and banking to ensure on hand petty cash was correctly managed. I even had the opportunity to train new staff and work across a variety of stores within the management cluster.

Due to my position, I had access to a store discount meaning I could dramatically increase the availability of any book titles I was interested in. Ultimately this led to being able to read a variety interesting fiction and nonfiction which I otherwise wouldn't have considered. Spending so much time in a book shop gave me insight into the world of literature. I developed my likes and dislikes and compounded on my already existing love of reading. I am grateful for the opportunity to have works alongside amazing colleagues to not only further my working abilities but also develop my personal taste in reading.

Key Skills Acquired

Team Work
Worked in a medium team to provide a positive customer experience and the best possible environment for the store.
Organisation
Manged back of house during christmas peak and developed new systems for organising the distribution of titles and items.
Logistics
Dispatched and received large and small deliveries to warehouses and customers alike.
Money handling
Provided banking assistance and general till discipline, working with large sums of money and ensuring accounting practices.

Technician, FixMyCrack Bristol

Working as a repair technician fix my crack has given me an opportunity to continue to develop my professional career during university. Fix my crack is a student led technical repair company focusing on screen and phone repairs. I work in collaboration with four other members of the Bristol team. Within the company there are five teams located at various universities across the country, all of us collaborating with each other. My job involves organizing repairs with customers and then repairing a broken device. I received training in a variety of phone models to prepare me for fixing a customers device. This Training has giving me an insight into the practical applications of electronics, and taught me the principals involved when dealing with expensive components and electronics.

Key Skills Acquired

Team Work
Worked in a small team to provide a positive customer experience and the best possible service to our customers.
Technically Trained
Underwent extensive training on the disciplines and intricacies of working with electronics.
Organisation
Personal accounting of hours and stock to be reported to superiors.
Logistics
Arranging times and places to coordinate with customers and carry out repair jobs.
